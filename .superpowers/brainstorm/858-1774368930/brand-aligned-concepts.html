Brand-aligned upsell concepts

Routine framing, not savings framing. Subtly luxurious, calm, expert. Savings visible but secondary.

A

Unified card with narrative subtext

Same card layout for both. Bundle gets a subtle teal left border and primary CTA. The message leads with the routine ("Kompletna ochrona na noc"), not the savings. Pricing shows bundle price + crossed-out original as a quiet anchor - no savings badge or callout.

Brand alignment: Routine framing per bundle strategy principle #4. Savings visible only as a quiet crossed-out price - the customer discovers the value, we don't shout it. "Subtelnie luksusowa, spokojna."

Strengths

  • Most compact - minimal vertical footprint in drawer
  • Visually unified - same card, same rhythm for both types
  • Teal border is subtle enough for premium positioning
  • Routine narrative leads, pricing secondary

Trade-offs

  • "+ 260 zl" button is small and might feel abrupt
  • The upgrade action (swap) isn't clearly communicated
  • Bundle pricing squeezed into the info column
B

Two-row card - product top, upgrade bottom

Cross-sell stays compact (identical to A). Bundle card extends with a quiet upgrade row below a hairline. The top shows the product (image, name, narrative). The bottom row has the action: narrative CTA label + price + button. Premium, unhurried, gives the customer room to process.

Brand alignment: The two-row layout mirrors premium fashion e-commerce (Net-a-Porter, Cuyana) where upgrade options sit below the product line, separated by whitespace. The customer processes what the bundle IS first, then considers the action. No urgency, no pressure - "spokojna."

Strengths

  • "Zamien na zestaw" clearly communicates the swap action
  • Customer processes product first, then action - less cognitive load
  • Upgrade row gives room for bundle contents + incremental price
  • Premium, unhurried feel - matches "spokojna" brand trait
  • Hairline separator is subtle, not aggressive

Trade-offs

  • Taller card takes more vertical space in drawer
  • Two visual patterns: compact cross-sell vs expanded bundle

Design decisions shared by both concepts

  • Routine framing leads - "Kompletna ochrona na noc" not "Oszczedzasz 109 zl"
  • Crossed-out price as quiet anchor - 508 zl in muted gray, customer discovers the value
  • No savings badge - per bundle strategy: savings secondary, never percentage
  • Incremental pricing - "+260 zl" not "399 zl" - the customer thinks "how much MORE" not "total price"
  • Product image for both types - visual anchor, premium feel
  • Same zone background - subtle off-white, contained within cart items column
  • Cross-sell identical - both concepts treat simple adds the same way
