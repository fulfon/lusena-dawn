Research-backed concepts

Gain-framed, brand-aligned, backed by Thaler's Mental Accounting + Zeigarnik Effect + our PDP patterns.

A

Gain-framed value card

Leads with what the customer GAINS: "Dodaj poszewke i zaoszczedz 109 zl." Shows the bundle image, name, savings chip (matching PDP), and price with crossed-out anchor. CTA says "Dodaj do zestawu" - the customer adds the missing piece, not pays extra.

Research basis:
  • Prospect Theory - gain framing ("zaoszczedz 109 zl") is 2x more motivating than loss framing ("+260 zl")
  • Gold savings chip - matches PDP pattern exactly (same color, same format)
  • Specific product name - "Dodaj poszewke" not vague "uzupelnij zestaw"
  • "Complete" framing - Zeigarnik Effect: the customer feels they're finishing something

B

Two-item "complete the set" card

Shows BOTH items visually: what they already have (with a check mark) and what they'd add. Applies Thaler's "fragment rewards" - they SEE both products and understand the set. Bottom row has pricing + savings chip + CTA. The customer processes the set composition FIRST, then considers the price.

Research basis:
  • Thaler's Mental Accounting - fragment rewards: showing both items separately maximizes perceived value (64% prefer seeing individual components)
  • Zeigarnik Effect - check mark on item they have + empty slot creates "incomplete set" tension
  • Anchoring - crossed-out 508 zl creates the reference; 399 zl feels like a smart decision
  • Brooklinen pattern - "everything you need" framing reduces cognitive load
