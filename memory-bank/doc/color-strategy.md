## Decision summary

| Color | Polish name | Hex range | Role |
|-------|-------------|-----------|------|
| Black | Czarny | `#1A1A1A` – `#2C2C2C` | Dark base |

**When matching to supplier palette:** Look for "Brudny róż" / "Dusty Rose" / "Mauve" / "Old Rose" / "Powder Pink" (muted, sophisticated — avoid hot pink, baby pink, salmon). Look for "Szampan" / "Champagne" / "Caramel" / "Warm Beige" (visible golden warmth — avoid metallic gold or flat beige). Black should be a true deep black that still shows silk sheen.

## Why these three colors

### Strategy: Unified capsule palette

Research on 20+ premium silk brands (Slip, Blissy, LILYSILK, Lunya, Kitsch, Bed Threads, Parachute, Brooklinen) found that every successful brand uses the same color palette across product lines. Not a single major silk brand uses different colors per product category. Reasons:

- **Bundles require color matching** — LUSENA's bundle strategy is a core revenue driver (see `bundle-strategy.md`)
- **Brand coherence increases revenue ~23%** — marketing research consensus on visual consistency
- **Tight palette signals "curated boutique"** — premium positioning, fewer colors = higher perceived value (Paradox of Choice research)
- **Instagram grid cohesion** — all products photograph together in a unified aesthetic
- **420 units / 10 SKUs** — per-product optimization would scatter inventory even thinner

### Color selection methodology

Colors were scored by actual bestseller data (Amazon review counts, Allegro offer counts, competitor sold-out signals, SENSILK HIT badges, ALMANIA bestseller indicators) across all 5 product categories independently. Cross-category performance determined the winners:

| Color | Pillowcase | Scrunchie | Bonnet | Eye Mask | Curler | Cross-Category |
|-------|-----------|-----------|--------|----------|--------|----------------|
| **Brudny róż** | #1 PL, #3 global | #2 | #2 | #3 | **#1** | **Best** |
| **Czarny** | #6 PL, #5 global | **#1** | **#1** | **#1** | #2 | **2nd** |
| **Szampan** | #3 PL (beige/cappuccino) | #3 | #3 | #4 | #3 | **3rd** |
| Biały/Ivory | #2 global | #5 (staining fear) | #4 (staining) | #5 (too light) | #4 | Weak cross-cat |
| Szary/Silver | #2 global, #4 PL | #4 | #4 | #4 | #4 | Mediocre |
| Granatowy | SENSILK HIT | #4 | #4 | #2 | #4 | Niche |

### Why Dusty Rose is the hero color (not just an accent)

Dusty rose is the ONLY color that appears in the top 3 for ALL 5 product categories:

- **Pillowcases:** #1 on Allegro by offer count (70x80 size). ALMANIA's Rose Gold is their confirmed bestseller.
- **Scrunchies:** #2 globally after black. Always included in multi-packs.
- **Bonnets:** #2 globally (14% of Allegro bonnet offers).
- **Eye masks:** #3 — the #1 gifted mask color (seasonal spikes at Valentine's/Mother's Day).
- **Curlers:** Dominant #1 — ~80% of viral TikTok curler videos feature pink products.

Critical: must be MUTED/DESATURATED. A 2025 study in the *Journal of Consumer Research* (Zhou et al., 7 experimental studies) found that low-saturation colors directly increase perceived luxury and willingness to pay. Consumers associate desaturated tones with heritage and quality. Hot pink = mass market. Dusty rose = premium.

### Why Black (not Grey) as the dark base

Black is #1 in 3 of 5 categories (scrunchies, bonnets, eye masks) and #2 in curlers. Grey is #4+ in every category in the Polish market specifically. Polish interior trends are actively moving away from cold greys toward warm tones (beige, mocha, cream — per Pantone/Benjamin Moore Poland 2025-2026 reports). Grey was in the original tentative palette but cross-category sales data does not support it over black.

Black also carries strong premium gift packaging associations in Poland (black box + gold accents = the default luxury packaging mental model).

### Why Champagne (not White) as the warm neutral

White/Ivory is #1 globally for pillowcases alone but weak cross-category:
- Staining fears reduce purchase intent for scrunchies and bonnets
- Functionally wrong for eye masks (too light)
- Higher return rates due to manufacturing tint variation (slight yellow/blue tints become obvious)

Champagne advantages:
- **Lowest return rates** of any silk color (warm neutrals are forgiving across screens)
- **Highest customer satisfaction ratings** on Amazon (4.5-4.7 vs 4.2-4.4 for black)
- **LoveSilk's Cappuccino** is their bestseller in Poland
- **Aligns with Mocha Mousse 2025 + Cloud Dancer 2026** Polish interior trends
- **No Polish competitor leads with champagne** — a market differentiator
- **Gift research:** "Gold turns any gift into a symbol of elegance" — serves LUSENA's gift positioning
- **Photographs beautifully** — shows silk luster without the stain/wear risk of white

## What was considered and rejected

| Color | Why rejected |
|-------|-------------|
| **Szary (Grey/Silver)** | #4+ in all Polish categories. Interior trend moving away from cold greys. "Safe but mediocre" — no category where it's the best choice. |
| **Biały (White/Ivory)** | #1 globally for pillowcases but terrible cross-category. Higher returns (tint variation). Staining fears for accessories. |
| **Granatowy (Navy)** | SENSILK's HIT for pillowcases, #2 for masks. But #4+ for scrunchies, bonnets, curlers. Best candidate for future expansion — see roadmap below. |
| **Butelkowa zieleń (Bottle Green)** | Available at competitors, nature/wellness trend alignment. Too niche for a 3-color launch. |
| **Lawendowy (Lavender)** | Bedroom trend, relaxation connotation, strong for 40-54 demo. Unproven in Polish silk market specifically. |
| **Bordowy (Burgundy)** | BOMOYE's primary color, fashion classic for AW season. Too seasonal and narrow for a cross-product capsule launch. |

## Color expansion roadmap

Priority order for adding a 4th color (data-gated — evaluate after 8-12 weeks of sales data):

1. **Granatowy (Navy)** — SENSILK's bestseller, #2 for masks, strong gift/trust associations, blue = #1 recommended bedroom color for sleep. Would go on: pillowcase, bonnet, scrunchie.
2. **Butelkowa zieleń (Bottle Green)** — rising trend (Pantone 2025-2026), all major PL competitors offer it, nature/wellness positioning aligns with LUSENA. Would go on: pillowcase, scrunchie.
3. **Lawendowy (Lavender)** — bedroom relaxation trend, strong for 40-54 female demographic, "future dusk" fashion trend in Poland. Would go on: pillowcase, scrunchie, bonnet.

**Decision trigger:** If a color sells through >70% in 45 days, restock and consider adding to more products. If <40% in 60 days, do not restock and test an alternative.

## Research basis

Research conducted 2026-03-20 across 4 independent streams (no priming from existing LUSENA documents — colors derived purely from cross-category sales data and market analysis):

1. **Competitor analysis (20+ brands):** Slip, Blissy, LILYSILK, Mayfairsilk, Fishers Finery, Brooklinen, Mulberry Park, Quince, ALMANIA, SENSILK, So Fluffy, BOMOYE, LoveSilk, Spadiora, Loczek, SilkyState, Kitsch, Lunya, Bed Threads, Parachute
2. **Color psychology:** Zhou et al. (2025) *Journal of Consumer Research* on saturation and luxury perception; Singh (2006) on color in purchasing decisions; Iyengar & Lepper (2000) Paradox of Choice; Frontiers in Psychology on gender/age color preferences; sleep research (Sleep Foundation, Simba)
3. **Polish market:** Allegro offer counts by color, Ceneo price comparison rankings, Polish interior trends (Pantone Color of the Year, Benjamin Moore Poland), fashion trends (Vogue Polska, Elle Polska), Polish gift packaging color associations
4. **Launch strategy:** DTC capsule collection case studies (Parachute, Brooklinen, Our Place, Bed Threads), SKU optimization research, inventory risk analysis, brand coherence revenue impact studies
