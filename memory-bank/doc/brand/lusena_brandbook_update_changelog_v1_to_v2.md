# LUSENA BrandBook Update Changelog (v1 → v2)

## Executive Summary (What’s New)

This update changes the brand’s center of gravity from a *single hero product (poszewka)* to a *portfolio-led “nocna rutyna”* and adds operational guidance for:

- Product hierarchy (Tier 1/2/3) and exposure rules
- Category-specific claims and expectation management (especially hair products)
- Bundling strategy aligned with the new hierarchy
- Email flows for cart recovery and post-purchase (social proof + LTV)
- A new Section 7: inventory/scaling playbook + launch social-proof strategy
- Expanded CS Playbook with the top hair/fit/“satyna vs jedwab” objections

If you used v1, focus first on:

1. `LUSENA_BrandBook_v.md:25` (positioning)
2. `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:89` + `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:97` (product tiers)
3. `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:350` (category messaging rules)
4. `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:1687` (bundles tables)
5. `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:968` (Cart Abandonment + Post-Purchase)
6. `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:1864` (new Section 7)

## Big Shifts vs v1

### 1) Positioning: “Poszewka” → “Kompletna nocna rutyna”

What changed:
- One-sentence positioning and elevator pitch now describe a full routine (skin + hair), not only pillowcase benefits.

Where:
- `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:25`

What to look for:
- Language that supports cross-sell (poszewka + gumki + czepki), and batch-level QC (“partia”) rather than unit-level.

---

### 2) Product Architecture: Add Tier Hierarchy + Exposure Rules

What changed:
- Product lines are restructured and **a strategic tier model** is introduced:
  - Tier 1 (Filar): poszewka 50×60 + scrunchies
  - Tier 2 (Wzrost): bonnet + hair curler
  - Tier 3 (Uzupełnienie): maska 3D
- Tier rules translate directly into: homepage order, paid focus, PLP sorting, and “what not to hero”.

Where:
- Product lines: `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:89`
- Tier table + rules: `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:97`

What to look for:
- Any merchandising/content decisions should reference “Tier” first (especially avoiding mask as a paid/homepage hero).

---

### 3) Core Spec Correction: 50×70 → 50×60

What changed:
- PDP naming example and bundle tables remove 50×70 and consistently use 50×60.

Where:
- PDP product name example: `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:1176`
- Bundles tables: `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:1687`

What to look for:
- Ensure Shopify products, assets, and any packaging/print text also match 50×60.

---

### 4) Verbal Identity: Category-Specific Promise Rules

What changed:
- New “rule 6” makes promises category-aware (skin care vs hair protection vs styling vs comfort).
- Added a whole section “Zasady komunikacji produktowej (wg kategorii)” that sets:
  - allowed vs forbidden claims
  - cross-sell copy guidance
  - expectation management (bonnet/curler risk of returns if “wow-effect” is promised)

Where:
- Tone rule 6: `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:247`
- Category rules: `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:350`

What to look for:
- Bonnet: promise “protection/maintenance”, not “metamorphosis”.
- Curler: explicitly manage expectations (hair type, humidity, time).

---

### 5) Risk Narrative: “Satyna ≠ Jedwab” (Educate, Don’t Attack)

What changed:
- Added a structured way to handle the dominant cheap-substitute objection across ads/PDP/CS.
- A new row is added to the “Mów / Rób / Nie mów” table.

Where:
- `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:65`

What to look for:
- The tone constraint: educate about material differences; do not shame customer choices.

---

### 6) Shopify Homepage: Tier-Driven Bestsellers + Bundles

- CTA options now point to collection/routine.

Where:
- `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:1245`

### 8) Email: Add Cart Abandonment + Post-Purchase Flows

What changed:

### 9) New Section 7: Inventory + Scaling + Social Proof Launch Strategy

What changed:
- A new, operational “Strategia Zatowarowania i Skalowania” section (Growth variant) with:
  - initial order allocation logic
  - triggers for phase transition and reorder discipline
  - a legal/compliant social proof plan for “0 reviews” problem

Where:
- Section 7 start: `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:1864`
- Social proof strategy: `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:2122`

What to look for:
- Concrete triggers and checklists you can turn into weekly ops.

---

### 10) CS Playbook Expanded (10 → 15 Scenarios) + New Rules

What changed:
- Added scenarios for the most likely hair-product objections and fit issues:
  - bonnet fit (slides/too tight/too loose)
  - “satynowy = taki sam” objection
  - curler “doesn’t work”
  - scrunchie stretching
  - foam mask comparison
- Added two general CS rules:
  - never attack cheap alternatives
  - manage expectations for Tier 2–3 products

Where:
- New CS Q11: `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:2322`
- New CS Q15: `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:2388`
- CS general rules: `LUSENA_BrandBook_v2.md:2404`

What to look for:
- Keep the “educate, don’t shame” pattern consistent across all channels.

## Quick Checklist for Teams Migrating from v1

- **Marketing/Copy**
  - Rewrite any “poszewka-only” messaging to “nocna rutyna”.
  - Apply category-specific promise rules (bonnet/curler expectation management).
  - Standardize “satyna ≠ jedwab” educational snippet.

- **E-commerce/Shopify**
  - Enforce Tier-driven homepage/PLP order.
  - Rebuild bundles and ensure 50×60 everywhere.

- **Customer Service**
  - Train on Q11–Q15 and the new “no attacking alternatives” rule.
  - Use the curler and bonnet scripts to reduce preventable returns.
